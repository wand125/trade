## Findings

- `250..260m` remains better than `480m` on drawdown/forced-exit behavior, but this does not matter enough when entry side is wrong.
- The current model is short-biased on fresh 2025-09..12 while the dense policy labels are long-favorable.
- The HGB side metrics alone are misleading: label balanced accuracy improves on test, but executable selected trades still have direction error rates around `0.55..0.72` and large EV overestimate versus realized PnL.
- Worst contexts repeatedly involve `short:london`, `short:rollover`, and `range_low_vol`.
- Standard policy should not switch to `260m` based on this result. The next main task is side calibration / side prior drift control, not further holding-cap tuning.
